Previously, we assumed that the player would submit only legal moves -- what should happen if the player enters an illegal move?

Updating the game loop

Once you have collected the row and column from a player's input, check to see if the row and column provided constitute a legal move. If so, handle as you did before. If not, print an error message and request fresh input. Continue this process until a legal move is provided.

You do not need to worry about the player entering gibberish (ie. "Xy47zQ 5R"). Assume they continue to enter two integers but do check these integers to verify that the row and column are on the board and the space is unclaimed.

You previously implemented a method to check against illegal moves. Use it.
